VAN DHAN VIKAS KENDRA

What is Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Yojana?

Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Yojana (PMVDY) is a government initiative. It was launched in 2016 to help tribal communities in India who depend on forest produce for their livelihood.
The scheme aims to create a sustainable livelihood for these communities. It provides them with financial support, training, and marketing support for their forest produce.

PMVDY has four components :

Van Dhan Clusters
Van Dhan Kendras
Van Dhan Vikas Kendra
Van Dhan Mission Directorate
The scheme has been implemented in 20 states and 2 Union Territories.
It has covered over 2.5 million tribal families.
The scheme has helped to generate over 10 million man-days of employment.
It has created over 10,000 Van Dhan Clusters and over 5,000 Van Dhan Kendras.
The scheme has helped to increase the income of tribal communities by over 20%.
It has helped to improve the marketing of forest produce and to reduce the exploitation of tribal communities.

Key Features of the Van Dhan Yojna

The project intends to help tribals participating in the accumulation of Minor Food Produces (MFPs) grow their economies by assisting them in making the best use of ecological assets and offering them a stable source of income.
Under this initiative, Van Dhan Vikas Kendras are built to offer capacity-enhancement training, including the creation of basic management and value-added facilities.

TRIFED has now launched “Sankalp se Siddhi” - Village & Digital Connect Drive. Starting from April 1, 2021, this 100 day drive will entail 150 teams (10 in each region from TRIFED and State Implementation Agencies/Mentoring Agencies/Partners) visiting ten villages each. 100 villages in each region and 1500 villages in the country will be covered in the next 100 days. The main aim of this drive is to activate the Van Dhan Vikas Kendras in these villages. Aim is to achieve Rs 200 crore Sales during the next 12 months from VanDhan units. The visiting teams will also identify locations and shortlist potential VDVKs for clustering as TRIFOOD, SFURTI units as larger enterprises.

To further the “Be Vocal for Local Buy Tribal” to create an “Atmanirbhar Bharat” call, TRIFED is working on the transformation from the Van Dhan mode to
Tribal enterprise mode. By clustering these Van Dhan Vikas Kendras into Van Dhan clusters and enterprises, the aim is to achieve economies of scale and promote high value addition produce.

To further institonalise the Van Dhan Vikas Kendra Clusters, TRIFED, under the aegis of Ministry of Tribal Affairs is driving convergences with different ministries and organisations. It has MoUs in place with different ministries such as MSME, MoFPI and Ministry of Rural Development to align the scheme with similar programmes of these Ministries, resulting in the convergence of Van Dhan Vikas Kendras and its clusters with SFURTI, ESDP from MSME, Food Parks Scheme of MoFPI and NRLM, within M/oRural Development.

These initiatives will be centred around the constitutional obligations under the Provisions of the Panchayats (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996 (PESA) and the Forest Rights Act, 2006 (FRA) to empower tribals living in the forest areas.
