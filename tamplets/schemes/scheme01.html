ADIWASI VIKAS YOJANA

Formation of Corporation

Gujarat Adijati Vikas Corporation is a statutory corporation. Which has been created from the Gujarat Tribal Development Act 1972 (Guj.Act No.5-1972). Government of Gujarat Notification No. GAH-5090-TDC-1012-J dt. 27-10-72 Thea Corporation came into autonomous existence. Dt. The Corporation has commenced operations with the first meeting of the Board of Directors held on 24/12/1972.

The Policy matters of the Corporation are decided by the Board of Directors.

Board of Directors :

The corporation consists of 9 members. In which 6 directors are non-government and 3 directors are government. 6 Bean Government Directors consist of 1 Chairman, 1 Vice Chairman and remaining 4 Directors. And 3 Government members include 1 Executive Director, 1 Deputy Subhiv (Tribal) and 1 Financial Adviser. Which is appointed by the state government.

Duties :

According to Section 16(1) of the Act, the tribal people living in Gujarat undertake the work of loans/credits for financial support. and undertakes the operations of recovery of loans made and all related proceedings.

Scope :

According to the provisions of the Gujarat Tribal Development Corporation Act 1972, the Corporation has been given a wide scope to carry out various activities for the social and economic welfare of the tribal people of the entire state of Gujarat. Gujarat Adijati Vikas Corporation was formed in the year 1972. At the time of the formation of the Corporation in the year 1972, the tribal population in Gujarat was approximately 3734422. And sponsorship offices existed in 9 districts in Gujarat. After that, according to the census of 2011, the total population of Gujarat is 60439692. In which the number of tribes is 8917174. Thus in approximately 39 years, the number of tribes has gradually increased. Thus about 14.75 percent of the total population belongs to tribals. And currently there are sponsorship offices in 14 districts out of total 33 districts of Gujarat.

It includes Palanpur, KhedBrahma, Modasa, Lunawada, Godhra, Dahod, Chotaudepur Rajpipla, Bharuch, Mandvi, Songarh, Vansada, Valsad and Dang. In these districts, the Corporation functions are carried out through the Sponsorship Administrator. Where one eighty of the corporation. A manager and a clerk do the work of the corporation. While in the non-tribal area, the corporation's operations are done through Vigilance Officer (Tribal).

According to Section 16(r) of the Act, on its own or as approved by the Board, in accordance with the primary duty of carrying out the work of social and economic upliftment of the members of the Scheduled Tribes residing in the State. Agricultural development program, marketing process, supply and storage of agricultural produce, small scale industries, construction of houses, transportation and other activities as approved by the State Government in this regard are to be organized in collaboration with or through tribal societies or other such agencies.

At present following schemes are implemented by the Corporation:

(1) Credit under Mudi Fund :

A capital fund of Rs.50 crore has been sanctioned by the State Government to undertake the activities of the Corporation. Which increased in 2017 to Rs. 65 crore has been made. Primarily from capital funds, the Corporation carries out lending activities at the rate of interest ranging from 9 percent to 14 percent under various schemes mentioned below by framing 39 different regulations.

(2) Personal credit to a member of a co-operative society:

From capital funds, the Corporation undertakes the task of approving credit proposals through cooperative societies for various purposes mentioned below under agriculture, animal husbandry and self-employment to tribal people who are members of cooperative societies. According to which the following objectives are stated. 1) Dairy cattle 2) Bullock carts 3) Bullock sheds 4) Oil engine 5) Electric motor 6) Fishing (net boat) 7) Sheep-goat-rabbit unit 8) For pipe-line 9) Blowing old wells 10) Sugar Share loan for members of industrial cooperative society 11) Institutional credit is provided under capital fund scheme for self-employment enterprises under total 39 regulations made by the corporation.
